Jeremy 'Fierto' Cruz

Hello I'm Jeremy Cruz also known as Fierto. I began tattooing in 2000. I opened Tenth Sanctum in January 2014. I enjoy doing traditional, neo traditional, illustrative, oriental, and black and grey portrait tattoos. I fashion myself as a focused tattooist paying close attention to detail. I think it's important to give my clients the best tattoo that I can do so that means at times I have to poke holes in ideas and concepts. This is not to be mean it's just so I can give you the best result. At the end of the day my main goal is to make you happy and at the same time not compromising the integrity of the tattoo as a whole. I enjoy spending time with my family, watching TV and movies of all kind. Omaha is my home and I am proud to live here and to be working with my friends.

Dave Koenig

Dave Koenig is a prolific painter and tattooist currently living and working in Omaha, Nebraska. His art has been exhibited at tattoo conventions, festivals and art galleries throughout the world including Into the Woods Fine Art Gallery in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, as well as The New BLK, In Common and Bemis Underground in Omaha, NE.  

After attending five years of fine arts at The University of Nebraska Omaha where he also studied Anthropology and Native American Studies, Dave then proceeded to teach art at Pact Center School in Council Bluffs, Iowa for a year followed by another year of teaching art at The St. Augustine School , located on the Winnebago Reservation. It was then that he began pursuing his career as a tattoo artist.  

Dave has been tattooing, both locally and internationally, since 2001. His home base is currently at Tenth Sanctum Tattoos in Omaha, Nebraska. Dave apprenticed under Devin Ferguson at Liquid Courage Tattoos. Other professional studios Dave has worked at include Sailor’s Grave, Iron Brush Tattoo, Studio XIII, True Love, Cast Iron Tattoo, Into the Woods, Off the Map and Arctic Tattoo in Tromso, Norway.  

Working with a wide range of mediums from Ink and water color to large scale acrylic, Dave maintains a controlled looseness of line work and a delicate treatment of color. Although heavily influenced by Japan’s historic culture and use of patterns, his work also reflects the imagery and antiquity of both Traditional Americana and Art Nouveau.

Dave has worked as curator for several art shows featuring fellow tattoo artists. His imagery and art have appeared on skateboards and t-shirts for several companies as well as several album covers, concert posters and mixed martial arts event posters. He is now expanding his work in to new territories of commercial art, painting custom guitars for Soapbox Guitars (of which he is co-owner) to illustrating a variety of liquor labels.  

“As for the future, I intend to roll with whatever life delivers, push forward and take my art beyond limits.” 

Client List: Curbside Clothing, Borgata Brewery, Soapbox Guitars

J Barnett

J Barnett has always worked in art related fields.  After Attending the Creative center and receiving a Associates degree in illustration and visual arts, he worked in the graphic design field for 7 years. It was not until receiving his first tattoo by Jeremy Cruz, that he considered tattooing as a career option.  After careful research J began his 2 year apprenticeship under Devin Fergusen in 2009.

Along with tattooing J also paints using a wide range of mediums. From large scale watercolors to Motorcycle tanks and helmets. His artwork has displayed at several art openings such as the New BLK, the Sweatshop, and several First Friday art events in which he also curated. J also has his own line of apparel at Curbside clothing/ curbsideclothing.com.

Open to all styles of tattooing and art, J feels he not only prefers, but excels at Oriental, Polynesian and American traditional.

    Tattoo Aftercare

    1. Wash your tattoo after 1-2 hours with HOT water and soap.
    2. Let the HOT water run over your tattoo for 5-10 min. (expanding the skin) DO NOT BURN YOURSELF.
    3. Pat Dry.
    4. Apply an ice pack to the newly tattooed area for 20 min. (contracting the skin and sealing in the newly colored goodness) Also, DO NOT APPLY ICE PACK DIRECTLY TO YOUR SKIN. Wrap it in a towel or cloth.
    5. Pat Dry.
    6. Leave the tattoo alone for 24 hrs.
    7. Apply skin lotion or Aquaphor as recommended 2-3 times a day. What ever skin lotion you prefer is fine. (Perfumed lotion contains alcohol and will sting a bit.)
    8. Continue using lotion on your tattoo forever and ever.
